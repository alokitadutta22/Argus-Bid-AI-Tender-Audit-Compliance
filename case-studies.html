Case study 02 · the responsive runner-up

Eligible, competitive, and beaten on the margins

Vendor archetype: strong, fully eligible bidder that simply offers less on the optional features.
85%
Rank 2
Responsive

The situation

A genuinely competitive vendor: a valid MAF, every statutory document, and all six mandatory specs met. The gap to first place is entirely in the preferred features.

What the engine found

  • ✓
    MAF valid and all eligibility gates cleared — fully responsive.
  • ✓
    All six mandatory specs met — the full 70 points.
  • !
    MACsec encryption not stated — recorded as [DATA LACKING], so it cannot be credited.
  • ×
    Warranty exactly 36 months — meets the mandatory floor, but does not earn the "beyond 36 months" preferred bonus.

How Argus Bid AI explained the ranking

Rank 1 (100%) edges out Rank 2 (85%) by 15 points. Decisive factors: Rank 1 satisfied "MACsec Encryption" while Rank 2 did not ([DATA LACKING]); Rank 1 satisfied "Extended OEM Warranty (>36m)" (60 months) while Rank 2 did not (36 months). On preferred features the leader met 4 vs 2 — the 30% preferred weighting separates them.
Why it matters: this is where evaluations get challenged. The runner-up did nothing wrong — it lost 15 points on two named, evidenced preferred features. Because the engine records exactly which parameters separated the two ranks, the committee can answer a vendor's "why did we come second?" in one sentence. Note too that a blank field becomes [DATA LACKING], never a silent assumption in the vendor's favour.
Case study 03 · the disqualification

Removed by the gates, before merit was ever scored

Vendor archetype: incomplete submission with a poor scan and an explicit deviation.
0%
Not ranked
Disqualified

The situation

Four documents, untidy filenames, one of them a low-quality scan. On a fast manual pass this bid might survive to scoring; Argus Bid AI's gates catch the eligibility failures immediately.

What the engine found

  • ×
    No MAF — no file classified as a Manufacturer's Authorization Form. Missing
  • ×
    Missing mandatory documents — PAN card, GeM registration, and audited balance sheet absent.
  • ×
    Pre-qualification failed — turnover below the INR 5 Crore floor; no GeM registration.
  • !
    Low-quality GST scan — readable enough to type, but flagged Low-Quality for human review.
  • !
    Explicit deviation detected — the bid offers 45°C-rated hardware against a 60°C requirement.

The auditable disqualification log

DISQUALIFIED — reasons logged: 1. Missing Mandatory Document — MAF 2. Missing Mandatory Document — PAN Card 3. Missing Mandatory Document — GeM Registration 4. Missing Mandatory Document — Audited Balance Sheet 5. Pre-Qualification Failure — Average Annual Turnover (< INR 5 Cr) 6. Pre-Qualification Failure — GeM Registration
Why it matters: eligibility comes before merit. Rather than scoring a bid that can never be awarded, Argus Bid AI removes it and records why, in numbered, clause-linked reasons. That log is the document that protects the evaluation if the disqualification is contested — and the low-quality scan is surfaced rather than quietly trusted.
